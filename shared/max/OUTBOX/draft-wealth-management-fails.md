# Why Traditional Wealth Management Fails Business Owners

## Blog Post (Highland) - Draft

### Hook
Most wealth managers have never run a business. Yet business owners trust them with their most valuable asset: the company itself.

### Outline

**1. The fundamental mismatch**
- Wealth managers: Trained for investable assets
- Business owners: Wealth is mostly illiquid (the business)
- Two completely different games

**2. What wealth managers get wrong**
- Don't understand business dynamics
- Treat the business as an afterthought
- Focus on diversification (when concentration is the real goal)
- No appreciation for operating leverage

**3. The business owner's reality**
- Single biggest asset = the business
- Need to optimize BEFORE exiting
- Business value != personal wealth
- Time is different (build vs. preserve)

**4. What works better**
- Business-focused advisors
- CFO-level thinking
- Exit planning specialists
- Integration with personal financial plan

**5. The better model**
- First: Optimize the business
- Then: Optimize personal wealth
- Always: Keep them connected

## LinkedIn Post

**Headline:** Why Traditional Wealth Management Fails Business Owners

**Body:**

Most wealth managers have never run a business.

Yet business owners trust them with their most valuable asset: the company itself.

That's a fundamental problem.

Wealth managers are trained to manage investable assets - stocks, bonds, mutual funds. They're great at asset allocation and diversification.

But for business owners, the business IS the wealth. And it's completely different game.

The mismatch:

📊 They see concentration as risk. You see it as strength.
📊 They see diversification as safety. You see it as underperformance.
📊 They see the business as one asset among many. You know it's everything.

What wealth managers get wrong:
- Don't understand business dynamics
- Treat the business as an afterthought
- Focus on the wrong priorities
- No appreciation for operating leverage

The solution:

First optimize the business. Then optimize personal wealth. Keep them connected.

That's what business-focused advising looks like.
