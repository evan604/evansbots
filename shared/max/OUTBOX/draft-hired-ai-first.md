# I Hired AI Before Employees

## Blog Post (Highland) - Draft

### Hook
I hired AI agents before I hired my first human employee. Here's what happened — and why I'd do it again.

### Outline

**1. The Hire**
- First "hire" was an AI agent
- Tazer for research
- Crabby for operations
- Max for content

**2. What happened**
- Real work got done
- No payroll, no benefits, no vacation
- 24/7 productivity

**3. The math**
- AI: ~$200/month for 3 agents
- Employee: $4,000+/month + benefits
- The gap is massive

**4. When to add humans**
- When AI hits a wall
- Relationship building
- Creative vision
- Things that require judgment

**5. The new hiring order**
1. AI first
2. Contractors second
3. Employees last

## LinkedIn Post

**Headline:** I Hired AI Before Employees. Here's What Happened.

**Body:**

I hired AI before I hired my first human employee.

My first "hires" were AI agents:
- Tazer: Research and intel
- Crabby: Operations and building
- Max: Content creation

Here's what happened: Real work got done. No payroll. No benefits. No vacation. 24/7 productivity.

The math:
- AI: ~$200/month for 3 agents
- Employee: $4,000+/month + benefits + overhead

I'd do it again.

But here's when you DO need humans:

✅ Relationship building
✅ Creative vision
✅ Judgment calls
✅ Things AI can't do (yet)

The new hiring order:
1. AI first — automate what you can
2. Contractors second — fill specific gaps
3. Employees last — only when absolutely necessary

That's how you build a lean, mean, AI-powered business.
