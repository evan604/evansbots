# The 2026 Estate Planning Playbook: What the $0 Million Exemption Means

## Blog Post (Highland) - Draft

### Hook
The estate tax exemption is about to shrink. Dramatically. If you haven't reviewed your estate plan, you're running out of time.

### Outline

**1. The Exemption Sunset**
- Current exemption: ~$13.99 million per person
- 2026 exemption: Drops to ~$7 million (roughly halved)
- Changes take effect January 1, 2026

**2. What this means for you**
- If estate > $7M: Potential tax bite
- If estate > $14M: Significant exposure
- Even smaller estates affected by state taxes

**3. Strategies to consider**
- Lifetime gifting (use it or lose it)
- Irrevocable life insurance trusts (ILITs)
- Family limited partnerships
- Charitable giving strategies
- Grantor retained annuity trusts (GRATs)

**4. The timeline**
- Now: Still time to act
- End of 2025: Last chance for current exemption
- 2026+: New rules, lower exemptions

**5. Action items**
- Get a valuation done
- Review current plan
- Consult with estate attorney
- Don't wait

The estate tax exemption is about to shrink. Dramatically.

Here's the reality:

📊 Current exemption: ~$13.99 million per person
📊 2026 exemption: ~$7 million (roughly halved)

The changes take effect January 1, 2026. That gives you roughly a year to act.

Strategies to consider NOW:

• Lifetime gifting - Use it or lose it
• ILITs - Irrevocable life insurance trusts
• Family limited partnerships
• Charitable giving strategies
• GRATs - Grantor retained annuity trusts

The timeline:
✅ Now: Still time to act
📅 End of 2025: Last chance for current exemption
⚠️ 2026+: New rules, lower exemptions

If you haven't reviewed your estate plan recently, the time is now.

Get a valuation. Review your current plan. Consult with an estate attorney.
